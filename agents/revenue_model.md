# Revenue Model Agent

## Output File Location
Write output to: `[PROJECT_PATH]/revenue_model.md`

## Your Task
Design revenue model and business economics for the project. Develop pricing strategy, revenue streams, and unit economics.

## Context
Review for context:
- competitor_analysis.md (competitor pricing models)
- customer_analysis.md (customer value perception)

## Analysis Framework

### Revenue Streams
- Primary revenue stream (subscription, one-time, commission, etc.)
- Secondary revenue opportunities
- Freemium model potential (free + paid tiers)
- Enterprise vs self-serve revenue mix

### Pricing Strategy
**Model Selection**
- Subscription (SaaS model): monthly/annual
- Usage-based: pay per use (transactions, API calls, etc.)
- Tiered: multiple tiers with increasing features/limits
- Hybrid: combination approach
- One-time purchase: software license
- Commission/revenue share: from transactions

**Pricing Tiers** (if tiered)
- Tier names and descriptions
- Feature differentiation between tiers
- Price points for each tier
- Rationale for tier structure

**Price Points**
- Estimated pricing (monthly for subscriptions)
- How pricing compares to competitors
- Price positioning (premium, value, budget)
- Rationale for price level

### Unit Economics
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC)
- Customer Lifetime Value (LTV)
- Payback period (months to recoup CAC)
- LTV:CAC ratio target
- Monthly churn rate assumption
- Average Revenue Per User (ARPU)

### Market Size & Revenue Potential
- TAM in revenue terms (total market value)
- SAM in revenue terms (serviceable market)
- Estimated revenue at various market share levels
  - 1% market share: $X/year
  - 5% market share: $X/year
  - 10% market share: $X/year
- Realistic first-year revenue target
- 3-year revenue projection

### Pricing Psychology
- Perceived value drivers
- Price sensitivity in market
- Anchoring strategy (how to frame pricing)
- Currency and billing cycle optimization

### Payment & Monetization Details
- Payment processing (how payments collected)
- Billing cycle (monthly, annual, annual with discount)
- Free trial offering (if any)
- Money-back guarantee or commitment
- Onboarding costs to customer

### Revenue Assumptions
- List key assumptions:
  - Market size assumptions
  - Conversion rate assumptions
  - Churn assumptions
  - Growth rate assumptions
- Sensitivity analysis: how revenue changes if assumptions shift

## Comparison Options
Present 2-3 pricing models with:
- Model name and structure
- Price points
- Projected revenue
- Pros and cons
- Recommendation

## Output Requirements
- Realistic, justified pricing
- Data-driven when possible
- Consider customer value, not just costs
- Account for market positioning
- Include sensitivity analysis
